Cultural heritage for future generations

Design team-project for preserving cultural heritage while making it more accessible to a wider audience of swedish tourists.

Challenge brief

With increasing digitalization, visitors expect more digitally enhanced experiences. In order for cultural heritage to appeal to a broader target group and to make cultural heritage sites more accessible and interesting for a wider age group, from children to the elderly, cultural heritage experiences need to be more engaging.

The challenge:
Create an interactive design solution that strengthen the understanding and experience of cultural heritage, with a focus on promoting a deeper connection to and understanding of historical places and objects.

The design solution should:

  • Convey cultural and historical information in a user-centered and visually appealing way.
  • Include interactivity features that strengthen tourist engagement and learning.
  • Adapt to both younger and older target groups, as well as tourists with varying technical skills.
  • Preserve and enhance the authentic feel and atmosphere of the cultural heritage, thereby enhancing the experience of it.

How can we create a digital experience to preserve Swedish Viking cultural heritage?

In Sweden, cultural heritage sites and monuments represent an invaluable connection to the past. However, modern audiences find traditional methods of presenting history uninteresting or inaccessible.

This project, "Cultural Heritage for Future Generations," aimed to address this challenge by leveraging augmented reality (AR) to create engaging, interactive, and memorable experiences for tourists. This initiative focused on making heritage sites more appealing and accessible while fostering a deeper connection to its history.

User Research

Going into this project we needed to develop an understanding about the barriers and opportunities for engaging swedish tourists with cultural heritage.

We sent out surveys to people of various ages. In total we collected 33 responses, predominantly aged 25-34, which revealed key barriers such as:

  • Low frequency of visits: 73.1% of respondents answered that they visit cultural and historical sites in Sweden at least once a year.
  • Lack of engaging storytelling & interactive features: 65.4% of respondents stated that they lack engaging storytelling and half of them would like a more interactive experience.
  • Digital tools to support learning & entertainment: More than half of the respondents prefer to learning through apps and digital tools, of which games and quizzes and Augmented Reality (AR) were suggested to enhance the experience.

Secondary research

We conducted a literature review and found that studies highlighted AR's potential to deepen users' connections to cultural heritage, and gamification can enhance the educational and interactive aspects of the experience. Key strategies included:

  • AR/VR (Sun & Wang, 2024; Hincapié et al., 2021; Koutsabasis et al., 2021; Wang et al., 2024)
  • Interactive narratives and storytelling (Wang et al., 2024; Koutsabasis et al., 2021)
  • Exploratory learning (Koutsabasis et al., 2021; Torsi, Ardito & Rebek, 2020)
  • Balancing education and and entertainment (Wang et al., 2024; Garcia et al., 2024)
  • Pervasive games: using the real world to create an immersive experience in a physical space (Torsi, Ardito & Rebek, 2020)

Ideation

We narrowed our design space to the use of AR-technology in cultural heritage experiences, specifically related to the Viking Age. Using a PACT analysis, we formulated How Might We (HMW) questions to guide us through the ideation phase:

  • HMW make the learning experience more engaging?
  • HMW make cultural heritage more accessible, to experience it both on-site and remotely?
  • HMW create an immersive experience that appeals to younger, as well as older tourists?

We also did some research into existing Viking Age AR-experiences in Sweden: Visir Uplandia, Augmented History: Gamla Uppsala, and Uppsala konst och kulturarv.

Design the right thing

Early-stage paper sketches, AR frames, and props allowed us to explore design possibilities without getting stuck on details. This allowed us to test out ideas with our target audience and explore how different functions engage people of varying ages, technical experience and interests.

Design the thing right

Feedback indicated a preference for interactive stories and AR features tied to physical sites (e.g., translating rune stones) over mini-game-style interactions. Later iterations included interactive AR elements and audio-guided narratives, focusing on the "look and feel" of the solution. These prototypes provided concrete feedback on usability and technical functionality.

Based on insights we revieved from user testing, we gathered requirements that the application should include:

  • Immersive narratives: stories that places users in the historical context of sites, fostering emotional connections.
  • Interactive features: rune stone translations & treasure hunt in AR, and quizzes to foster learning and exploration.
  • Reward system: points, collectable items, and levels, encouraging continued engagement.
  • Accessibility: providing an equal experience both for on-site users and remote users.
