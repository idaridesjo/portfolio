Guilty pleasure - book room

Designing to enhance guilty pleasure-experiences through exploratory prototyping.

Overview

The project focused on designing an interactive solution that addressed the concept of guilty pleasures in digital experiences. The target audience consisted of avid audiobook listeners, particularly those engaging with niche genres such as romantasy, who desired a sense of community and deeper immersion in their listening experience. The goal was to create a design that fostered social connection while also enabling personal enjoyment in a safe, customizable environment.

Definition of guilty pleasure

"A guilty pleasure is any thought, activity, or behavior that is personally enjoyable while also evoking feelings of shame or embarrassment." (Drew, 2023)

Designing the right thing

The project began with gaining understanding about how people engage with their guilty pleasures. Research highlighted that guilty pleasures are often deeply personal and highly gratifying experiences that bring people joy—especially when shared socially (Benyon, 2019; NPR, 2024).

The initial phase prioritized exploratory prototyping. This stage involved sketches and paper prototypes to generate insights into user behaviors, needs, and hidden motivations. Specifically, I sought to explore:

  • How people currently engage with their guilty pleasures (e.g., sharing thoughts, emotions, and experiences).
  • The social dimensions of guilty pleasures.
  • How technology could facilitate a pleasurable and meaningful experience while respecting the personal nature of guilty pleasures.

Exploratory prototyping

A participatory design approach involving users engagement throughout all phases of the design process, through exploratory prototyping, role-playing and probes.

Findings

The exploratory prototypes were essential in uncovering key insights about why people engage in guilty pleasures. The most common reasons were: a temporary escape from reality/responsibilities, and a source of relaxation or self-care.

I chose to narrow down the design space focusing on the guilty pleasure reading or listening to books that's considered smutty/taboo, including genres such as romantasy, erotica, dark romance etc.

People read because it brings them: joy, comfort, relaxation, or a sense of escape, need to be able to share their interests with others without feeling shame or fear of being judged. They want to be a part of community for the following reasons:

  • A sense of belonging — reading together instead of reading alone gives a sense of belonging and connection.
  • Self expression — a place to freely discuss and share thoughts about books.
  • Like-minded people — meeting people with similar interests.

Brainwriting

Based on the insights above, I had a brainwriting-session with a potential user in order to come up with ideas for design solutions. We used Miro to write ideas on post-its and then we voted on the two best ideas. Which we continued to ideate on and build of each others ideas. We came up with the idea of "book rooms" where people can read/listen to books together.

Experience prototyping

To test out the idea of a virtual audiobook room, and how/if this can creating a sense of community and belonging. To help create a realistic scenario of the user's potential experience, I facilitated a test via Zoom to simulate its context of use (i.e. a digital environment where users can listen to their guilty pleasure-books together).

This prototype allowed me to explore the role of the design solution and its potential value for users, while also contributing to insights into which functionalities and interaction possibilities they find meaningful and how these can enhance their experience.

Sketches

I started sketching out the flow of discovering a book room, joining a room, and interactions within the book room.

Design the thing right

I moved on to creating a low-fidelity prototype, which aimed to validate the core functionality and structure of the application.

  • Book Rooms: Virtual spaces where users could join others reading or listening to the same book or genre.
  • Chat Functionality: Enabling users to discuss and share thoughts on books in real-time.
  • Bookmark Sharing: Allowing users to annotate specific moments in an audiobook and choose whether to keep them private or share them with others in the Book Room.

User testing with participants revealed that chat and bookmark-sharing feature was appreciated for its reflective and social potential, but they want to be able to turn off elements may be distracting, e.g. chat, emojis.
